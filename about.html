Biography

Noah was born and raised in Arizona, USA. He started learning violin when he was five and quit shortly thereafter. Then, in 5th grade when his school offered a band program, he played clarinet for a year and then switched to oboe. He is now an acclaimed student oboist in the state. Shortly after beginning to play clarinet, his interest in music blossomed and he discovered the free music notation software, MuseScore, when he was 10. From ages 10 to 13, most of his creations were unsubstantial and unimpressive. However, his opus 1 piece, Serenade in A Minor for 2 flutes and oboe, got good reception from his band teacher and fellow members of his school trio. This inspired him to compete in the National Young Composers Challenge for the 2022 Composium. The piece he wrote for this competition, Carnival Waltz, was a big boost to his up-and-coming career, and caught the attention of Dr. Temme, the conductor of the Phoenix Youth Symphony Orchestra’s Philharmonic Orchestra. This led to the performance of his Carnival Waltz—his world premiere—on February 27th, 2022.

Usage and Copywright of my Work

All music and the scores and parts for them are under a Creative Commons BY-SA 4.0 license. This means that you can do whatever you want with the content (including performances and recordings) as long as you give me credit. A commission for these uses is nice, but not required as of right now.


Terms and Conditions of Commissions

I do not charge for commissions at this time. This is because the exposure of having an ensemble perform one of my works is payment enough.
When you commission a piece, I will ask (if applicable) if I have your permission to publish the live recording of the piece on YouTube, Spotify, etc. You do not have to say yes to this to commission a piece, it is just helpful for me attracting new clients.
Also, I will still have full rights to the piece, just not the rights to your specific recording or video of the performance.
